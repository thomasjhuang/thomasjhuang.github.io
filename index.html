Waitless

An awesome Facebook Hackathon product

January 24th, 2014

Project

24 Hour hackathon to build something cool. We decided to build a mobile solution that solves our major life annoyance as students - line ups.
The prototype app is live at http://waitless.meteor.com

Some context to things...

Bonnie, Joe and I gathered to make this. half an hour before the hack we had no idea what to do. Bonnie then said, you know what annoys me the most? Having to line up for coffee at Tim Hortons everyday. An idea sparked, we are making Waitless.

Umm.. What is Waitless?

Waitless is a problem solver. It is an online solution that lets you order your favourite drink at your nearest locations and pick them up when the order is ready at the time you chose. Try is out and you will see how simple yet effective it is.




So... what happened?

Bonnie and Joe are awesome developers. I was happy to be the designer. Every single view of the app was mocked up, wireframed, and coded during the hack. There was quite a number of iterations and debugging but I was able to pull out a complete front end by 5am in the morning (Hack started at 6pm)

A quick breakdown of the app, it contained the following views:

    If you're a user

  • Login

  • Dashboard - contains all goodies

  • Profile/Settings

  • Buy drink - 1. location 2.drink type 3.time

    If you are a vendor

  • Login

  • Dashboard - view all upcoming orders

    • View all upcoming order

    • View lastest incoming order






    • Buying steps




Lastly...

Well it was 24 hours of sleepless programming and designing, I have to say it I really enjoyed it. Props to Bonnie, Joe and Constance, it was definitely some intense teamwork and it was a thrill to work with all of them.

WaterlUX

A Friday Night Coding Date Project

January 17th, 2014

Project

To design a simple website for WaterlUX - a student club for UI/UX enthusiasts

Some context to things...

Like all non-typical fridays, Clarisse and I decided to go on a date, not to the bars but to code. I said okay.
WaterlUX is a UWaterloo club that gathers around people who are into UX design. Its a place to learn, talk, and collaborate.

We needed a website, and a pretty one. My usual routine is to first gather cool ideas and mock up the template first in photoshop. But since we wanted to push the site asap, as soon as we decided on the section contents, I started coding right away.






So... what happened?

We decided on page slide, because each section contains decent amount of material that can have its own page. We wanted parallax to give the site some more flings, and finally, teal theme was what we went with. We started around 9pm on Friday, the site was completed in about 5 - 6 hours. It was a perfect Friday night date.



Lastly...

Many lovely thanks to Clarisse Schneider as this project wouldn't have taken off without her brilliant ideas and dedication for WaterlUX. I definitely enjoyed this project as I not only learned how to code under pressure, but also to get to know a great person and a very dear friend.

The page slide function created was sweet - but it got rid of the parallax effect and it is not compatible with larger screens, I am still looking into the matter and trying to figure out the most optimal solution towards it. Stay tuned!
